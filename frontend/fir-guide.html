FIR Guide

FIR registration is FREE. Follow these steps in Tamil Nadu.

What is an FIR?

A First Information Report (FIR) is the first document prepared by police when they receive information about a cognizable offence. It sets the criminal law in div.

Steps to File an FIR

  1. Visit the nearest police station (any station for Zero FIR in serious cases).
  2. Provide your name, address, and detailed account of the incident with date, time, and place.
  3. Names of accused (if known) and names of witnesses.
  4. Police must register FIR for cognizable offences — they cannot refuse.
  5. Ask for a free copy of the FIR. Note the FIR number for future reference.
  6. If refused, approach the Superintendent of Police or Magistrate.

Zero FIR

Any police station can register a Zero FIR for offences including those against women, and transfer it to the correct jurisdiction. Women can file at any station regardless of location.

Documents to Carry

  • Government photo ID (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport)
  • Medical reports (if applicable)
  • Written complaint copy (optional but helpful)
  • Evidence: photos, messages, CCTV details
